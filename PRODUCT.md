# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

CosmediLoans serves Australian patients researching or preparing to fund medical, dental, fertility, cosmetic, veterinary, and elective treatment. They are often comparing procedure costs, worried about affordability, and looking for a fast, low-friction way to understand whether financing is possible before booking care.

Primary users need confidence, speed, and plain-language guidance. They want to compare options without feeling trapped by a single bank, a buy-now-pay-later offer, or a hard credit check too early in the journey.

## Product Purpose

CosmediLoans is a lead-generation and broker-matching website for medical procedure financing in Australia. It captures patient enquiries and connects them with licensed finance brokers who compare 20+ lenders for a suitable rate.

The site should make medical financing feel understandable, transparent, and approachable. Success means more qualified lead submissions, stronger organic traffic from procedure and comparison pages, and enough trust for a visitor to check their rate without feeling pressured.

CosmediLoans should not present itself as a lender, credit provider, clinic, or financial advice provider. It should clearly communicate that patients are matched with brokers and lender options.

## Brand Personality

Clear, trustworthy, empowering.

The brand should feel clinically professional without becoming cold. It should carry the reassurance of a serious financial service, the warmth needed for sensitive medical decisions, and the directness of a high-converting lead-capture site.

The emotional target is confidence: the visitor should feel that they have a practical path from treatment cost anxiety to a realistic funding option.

## Anti-references

CosmediLoans must not feel like a cold bank site, a payday-loan or BNPL gimmick, a crypto or neon fintech dashboard, or a generic medical clinic.

Avoid generic SaaS landing-page cliches, dark-mode finance tropes, purple gradients, over-polished stock imagery, fear-based urgency, and anything that makes sensitive medical procedures feel transactional or exploitative.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with clarity before persuasion. Visitors should understand the service, the broker-matching model, and the soft-credit promise before they see aggressive conversion language.
2. Make trust visible in every major section. Broker matching, 20+ lenders, no patient fee, soft credit checks, Australian context, and compliance disclaimers should be easy to find.
3. Keep medical topics human and respectful. Procedure pages can be conversion-focused, but the tone must stay sensitive to fertility, cosmetic, dental, veterinary, and elective care decisions.
4. Use comparison as the core value. The site should consistently reinforce that CosmediLoans helps patients avoid single-provider lock-in by comparing lender options.
5. Convert with low friction. Forms, calculators, sticky CTAs, and inline lead capture should feel quick, safe, and optional rather than pushy.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Preserve strong color contrast, visible focus states, semantic headings, accessible form labels and errors, and keyboard-friendly navigation.

Support reduced-motion preferences, avoid motion that blocks comprehension, and keep all touch targets at least 44px on mobile. The site should remain usable for color-blind users without relying on color alone for meaning.
